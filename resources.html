Getting Started Guide

Welcome to SolarConnect! Follow this guide to get your account set up and start trading energy.

Step 1: Sign Up and Create Your Profile

Begin by creating your account on our Sign Up page. You'll need to provide some basic information and select your user type (Consumer, Producer, or Prosumer).

Step 2: Connect Your Solar System

If you're a producer, you'll need to securely connect your solar energy system to our platform. Our system uses a smart meter to track your energy generation and export. For a hassle-free setup, we recommend a certified solar contractor to ensure your system is properly connected to the grid and our network.

Step 3: Set Your Preferences

Configure your preferences to automate trades. Consumers can set a price limit, minimum energy amount, and preferred producers. Producers can set their price per kWh and manage their energy listings. This allows the platform to automatically match you with the best available trades.

Step 4: Monitor and Trade

Use your personal dashboard to monitor your energy consumption, generation, and savings. The dashboard provides real-time data and a complete trade history, giving you full control and transparency over your energy usage.

How to Price Your Energy

Setting a competitive and profitable price is key to a successful experience as a producer on SolarConnect. Consider the following factors:

Understanding Your Costs

Your energy price should be based on your **Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE)**. This is the total cost of your solar system over its lifetime, divided by its total energy output. Factors include:

  • **Equipment Costs:** Solar panels, inverters, and mounting structures.
  • **Installation Costs:** Labor, permits, and grid-connection fees.
  • **Operating & Maintenance (O&M):** Annual cleaning, monitoring, and repairs.

Market and Grid Pricing

Research the average **price per watt (₹/W)** in your local market. Compare your LCOE to your utility's residential electricity rate to ensure your price is competitive. You can offer a small discount to attract more buyers while still earning a profit.

Maximizing Your Profit

The **price per kWh** is the main factor consumers consider. A lower price can attract more buyers, while a higher price increases your profit margin per trade. Your goal is to find a balance. Using our platform's real-time data, you can adjust your price based on current demand and available supply to maximize your earnings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do solar panels work?

Solar panels are made of photovoltaic (PV) cells that absorb sunlight and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter then changes this DC power into alternating current (AC), which powers your home or business appliances. Excess energy can be stored in a battery or sold directly to other users on our platform.

What is a Virtual Power Plant (VPP)?

A Virtual Power Plant (VPP) is a network of distributed energy resources like residential solar panels and batteries that are aggregated and managed by a central control system. SolarConnect's platform acts as a VPP, allowing us to manage and trade energy between users, ensuring grid stability and efficiency.

Do solar panels work on cloudy or rainy days?

Yes, solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy or rainy days, but their efficiency is reduced. They use diffused sunlight to produce power, though the output may be 30-50% lower compared to a sunny day. Your home can draw from the grid or a battery on days when solar production is low.

How do I maintain my solar panels?

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Regularly cleaning the surface (every 2-3 months) to remove dust and debris will ensure maximum efficiency. We recommend a professional check-up once a year to inspect the wiring, inverter, and overall system health.
